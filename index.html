Cutting Edge Cuisine Just Not Where You'd Expect

Food Reframed is a unique travel and food show based on the premise that cutting edge cuisine and food innovation can come from anywhere: from an inventive grandmother, inspired aunt or a visionary farmer, as much as from a trained professional chef. Experimentation, new techniques and even food revolutions can be found in the streets as well as in restaurants, in a village as much as in a city, and across every environment, culture and socio-economic class.

With the spontaneity of live field production and the perspicacity of well-researched journalism and an awareness of global food, history and culture, this show will discover food innovation in unexpected places, from radically new flavor combinations and uses of ingredients to revolutions in food production and sustainability. Exploring destinations well off the foodie traveler path, we’ll meet maverick food creators who are building on or breaking from tradition. With a focus on the progressive/modern rather that on the traditional / exotic, this show promises to be the most insightful and relevant food and travel show on the planet.

Countless TV food shows have made household names out of creative chefs and restaurants in famous foodie capitals like New York, LA, London, Paris, Bologna and Melbourne. Yet when it comes to less mainstream destinations, we only get to see the traditional and the “weird” – never the modern and innovative.

This is the untold story of modern cuisine, where you don’t need Michelin stars for your talent to be recognized.

Innovation: From Home Cooking to Fine Dining

Discover new places, people and tastes

Food Reframed will be a series of 42 min episodes for travel, food and lifestyle channels, discovering unconventional and obscure destinations. in each episode the host will visit a different country, city or region, discovering food innovation of all kinds, whether it’s coming from a maverick chef, an eccentric home cook, an audacious street vendor or an enterprising primary producer. The show will constantly surprise the viewer by challenge common stereotypes and preconceptions of non-Western cultures and communities.

A typical episode will have have 6-7 segments with local guides and guests, including local chefs, cooks, restaurateurs, writers and foodies, who will reveal local secrets and help the host understand the location’s history, pop culture and traditions, providing a context for our food journey and the characters we meet.

The show will also explore how food innovation is transforming communities and making people more independent.

How are we different? Why now?

The shows that set the groundwork for the “Food & Travel” genre are Anthony Bourdain’s No Reservations (2005 – 2012) and Parts Unknown (2013- present) and Andrew Zimmern’s Bizarre Foods (2006 – present). For ten years these pioneers have been educating and broadening the horizons of their audience, leaving it hungry for more. Today, with no animal organ, insect or fermented product left uneaten on TV, the audience is much more sophisticated, well traveled and aware than a decade ago.

Food ReFramed promises to take the format to the next level, with a fresh approach and attitude, celebrating food innovation, alternative takes on modernity, and unexpected ways in which food ideas can change lives, communities and even entire cities and countries.

Style

Combining the dynamic spontaneity of live field production with the perspicacity of researched reportage journalism, episodes will focus some of the world’s least visited countries, and places not known for their cuisine. Each episode will feature 5-7 food segments as well as essential cultural, geographic, political and historic context.

Host: Gabe Kennedy

The show will be aimed at all lovers of food and travel, presented by an perceptive and down-to-earth host who has a deep understanding of food, a passion for world cultures and history, an appreciation of new ideas, creativity and eccentricity, as well as an open mind, a genuine love of people and a sense of humor.

Chef Gabe Kennedy is one of the most exciting new figures on the New York food scene. A winner of “The Taste” on ABC, he has been mentored by the likes of Marcus Samuelsson and Anthony Bourdain, and he has a passion for understanding cuisines of the world and for using food as a way to change lives. In this documentary, we join Gabe on his travels to Zimbabwe, an environment as far as one can get from New York, with its obsession with novelty and choice. On this journey he will join forces with chefs and cooks, restaurateurs, food producers, people from the local media and other Zimbabweans who against impossible odds are reclaiming and sharing their stolen culinary history, in ways that could potentially transform the lives of millions of people.
